Understanding Music Through Analysis

This page examines five influential songs across different genres to demonstrate how musical elements combine to create specific emotional responses. Each analysis supports the scientific principles discussed throughout this website.

Someone Like You

by Adele
RELAX

Context & Background

  • Written by Adele and Dan Wilson with a simple piano arrangement
  • Won Grammy Award for Best Pop Solo Performance in 2012
  • First piano ballad to enter the Billboard Hot 100
  • The ballad-like structure and piano create a haunting resonance

Musical Analysis

Tempo & Atmosphere

The slow tempo creates a calm atmosphere that supports self-contemplation. Research shows slower music is associated with reflective emotions and sadness. Adele's vocal timbre is rich, warm, and deep — conveying vulnerability.

Lyrical Content

The lyrics show acceptance after emotional loss.

Lyric evidence: "Never mind, I'll find someone like you."

This shows adaptation to acceptance rather than anger — reinforcing a calm, emotional tone.

Piano Instrumentation

The song relies mostly on piano with little else. This simple tone prevents emotional overload, allowing the lyrics to convey their sad message without distraction.

Why This Song Fits the Website

  • Fits the Relax category — regulates emotions with a calming melody
  • Strong lyrical content makes it not ideal for studying, demonstrating how lyrics differ from instrumental music for tasks

Happy

by Pharrell Williams
HAPPY & ENERGISED

Context & Background

  • Released in 2013 as part of the Despicable Me 2 soundtrack
  • Reached number one in twenty countries worldwide
  • Earned an Academy Award nomination for Best Original Song
  • Uses energetic instruments and cheerful lyrics to evoke joy and motivation

Musical Analysis

Tempo & Energy

Fast tempo and steady groove increase energy level. Music psychology shows faster tempos are linked to positive emotions and happiness.

Major Key & Harmony

Written in a major key which is perceived as joyful. Major harmonies reduce emotional tension and promote a positive mood.

Lyrics & Emotional Meaning

Lyrics focus on freedom and joy, encouraging listeners to feel engaged and positive.

Lyric evidence: "Because I'm happy, clap along if you feel like a room without a roof."

Why This Song Fits the Website

  • Fits the Happy and Energised categories — increases emotional engagement and promotes positive mood
  • Strong lyrical presence means it isn't meant for studying, supporting the difference between emotional and focus music

Don't Stop Me Now

by Queen
HAPPY & ENERGISED

Context & Background

  • Rock song written by Freddie Mercury, released in 1978 on the album Jazz
  • One of Queen's most popular and well-known songs
  • Strong rhythm and expressive vocals make it effective at evoking excitement

Musical Analysis

Fast Tempo & Driving Rhythm

Fast tempo increases arousal and excitement. Research shows fast-paced music is strongly associated with motivation and physical energy.

Lyrics & Emotional Meaning

Lyric evidence: "I'm having such a good time, I'm having a ball."

These lyrics express pure joy, reinforcing the song's happy emotional tone.

Instrumentation & Rock Style

The combination of drums, bass, piano, and electric guitar creates an energetic sound that helps sustain excitement.

Why This Song Fits the Website

  • Fits the Happy and Energised categories because of its fast tempo, major key, and joyful lyrics
  • Strong rhythm and lyrical presence means it isn't ideal for studying or focusing
"Rock music's fast tempo and strong rhythm make it especially effective for increasing energy and positive mood."— American Psychological Association

Time

by Hans Zimmer
RELAX / REFLECTIVE

Context & Background

  • Instrumental film score composed for the 2010 movie Inception
  • One of Zimmer's most recognisable works, used in wide variety of media
  • Focuses on atmosphere buildup and emotional depth without lyrics

Musical Analysis

Gradual Build-Up

The song begins slowly and builds over time, allowing the listener to feel emotional anticipation. Research shows gradual pieces create emotional depth rather than immediate happiness or sadness.

Instrumental (No Lyrics)

No lyrics prevents language processing, allowing the listener to focus on emotional thinking. Research shows instrumental music encourages emotional reflection without cognitive overload.

Orchestral Instrumentation

Piano, strings, and layered orchestrated textures. Strings are strongly linked to emotional expression and tension.

Why This Song Fits the Website

  • Fits the Relax/Reflective category — demonstrates how music evokes deep emotion without lyrics
  • Not ideal for studying due to its emotional build-up, but effective for emotional reflection

Clair de Lune

by Claude Debussy
FOCUS

Context & Background

  • Piano composition published in 1905 as part of Suite bergamasque
  • One of the most famous classical works worldwide
  • Used frequently in study playlists — fully instrumental and harmonically smooth

Musical Analysis

Tempo & Structure

Moderate, steady tempo with gradual changes. Studies show music with slow to moderate tempos and predictable phrasing supports concentration and reduces distraction.

Absence of Lyrics

No lyrics means verbal processing doesn't compete with thinking tasks. Studies show lyrics activate language regions of the brain which reduces task efficiency.

Listener Response

Clair de Lune is included in many "Focus" and "Deep Work" playlists. Students use music like this while studying or writing.

"Clair de Lune helps me stay focused because it keeps my mind calm without distracting me."— Spotify user review

Why This Song Fits the Website

  • Fits the Focus category — balances emotional warmth with cognitive neutral thinking
  • Absence of lyrics and smooth harmonic progression make it suitable for studying
  • Demonstrates the difference of instrumental music from lyrical music in its effect on productivity
